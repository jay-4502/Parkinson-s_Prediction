Motor symptoms and Machine Learning

Parkinson’s disease symptoms can affect people in different ways and there are many different symptoms associated with the condition. One of the most recognised signs of Parkinson’s disease is the tremor - shaking that usually begins in the hand or arm. But a lesser known sign of Parkinson’s is a change in a person’s handwriting.

Early Detection

Changes in handwriting and voice patterns can manifest in the early stages of Parkinson's disease, sometimes before other noticeable symptoms. Machine Learning focuses on detecting Parkinson's disease in its early stages, allowing for timely intervention and treatment.

Non-Invasive & Low-Cost

The methods are non-invasive and cost-effective, making them accessible to a wider population for regular monitoring.

Objective Assessment:

Machine learning algorithms can provide an objective assessment of handwriting and voice patterns, eliminating potential biases that may arise in subjective assessments by healthcare professionals.

Quantifiable Features

Handwriting and voice patterns contain quantifiable features that can be extracted and analyzed using machine learning techniques.
